Energy Consumption Data Visualisations

This dashboard shows interactive visualisations built using D3.js. The charts explore energy consumption patterns in televisions and electricity price trends.

Energy Consumption vs Star Rating

This scatter plot shows the relationship between Energy Star rating and the annual energy consumption of televisions. In general, TVs with higher star ratings tend to consume less energy, although there is still considerable variation within each rating group. This suggests that other factors such as screen size and display technology also influence energy usage. Some low-rated TVs consume more than 2000 kWh per year, while higher-rated TVs cluster at much lower energy levels.

Proportion of TV Models by Screen Technology across all TVs combined

The donut chart illustrates the proportion of TV models by screen technology in the dataset. LCD (LED) televisions dominate the market, accounting for more than 80% of all models. Traditional LCD TVs make up around 11%, while OLED represents a smaller but growing segment. Plasma TVs appear only rarely in the dataset, indicating that this technology is largely being phased out of the modern television market.

Energy Consumption for different screen technologies for 50inch TVs only

This bar chart compares the average energy consumption of different screen technologies for televisions with a 50-inch screen size. Plasma TVs consume the most energy on average, reaching nearly 500 kWh per year. In contrast, LCD, LCD (LED), and OLED technologies show lower consumption levels, with OLED and LCD models being the most energy-efficient among the four technologies.

Trend of average power prices from 1998 to 2024

The line chart shows the trend of average electricity prices from 1998 to 2024. Prices remained relatively stable during the early 2000s, followed by several periods of fluctuation. In recent years, electricity prices have increased significantly, reaching their highest levels after 2020. This upward trend highlights the growing importance of energy-efficient appliances, including televisions.
